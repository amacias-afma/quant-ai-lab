1 The Challenge

Volatility is Invisible.

Value at Risk (VaR) is the "Capital Shield" banks must hold to survive crashes. If the shield is too thin, the bank fails. If it's too heavy, capital is wasted.

The Problem: Traditional models (GARCH) assume returns are "Normal" (Gaussian). Real markets have "Fat Tails" (Crashes happen 100x more often than predicted).

2 The "Aha!" Moment

The Student-t Fix

Initially, our LSTM model failed with an 11% Breach Rate (Target < 1%). Why? Because it learned the variance ($\sigma^2$) but not the kurtosis (Tail Risk).

The Solution: We abandoned the Standard Normal ($Z=2.33$) and implemented Empirical Calibration. By mapping the LSTM residuals to a Student-t distribution, we derived a dynamic multiplier ($Z \approx 3.1$) that captures the "Fear" in the market.
